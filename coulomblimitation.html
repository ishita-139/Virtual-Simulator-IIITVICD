Properties

There are the following properties of coulomb's law:-
Coulomb force is action and reaction pair and follows Newton's third law.
Coulomb force is a conservative force.
Coulomb force is central force i.e. it is always acting along the line joining between two charges.
If the net force is zero then momentum will be conserved.
If the center of mass at rest and momentum is conserved then it follows the mass conservation law.
The force between two charges is independent of the presence or absence of other charges but the net force increase on that particular charge.

Limitation

The law is applicable only for the point charges at rest.
Coulomb’s law can only be applied in those cases where the inverse square law is obeyed.
It is difficult to implement Coulomb’s law where charges are in arbitrary shape because, in such cases, we cannot determine the distance between the charges.
The law can’t be used directly to calculate the charge on big planets.

Applications

Coulomb’s law is the most fundamental law of electrostatic physics, the following are some major applications where coulomb’s law is used for calculations:
Coulomb’s law calculates the electrostatic force acting between the point charges.
Coulomb’s law also determines the distance between two point charged bodies.
Coulomb’s law is also used to determine the electrostatic force acting on a point charge due to a number of charges.
