Application

Logic gates are basic building blocks of digital electronics and are used in a wide variety of electronic devices and systems. Some real-life examples of logic gates include:
Computer processors: The central processing unit (CPU) of a computer uses logic gates to perform logical operations on data.
Memory devices: Memory devices such as RAM and ROM use logic gates to store and retrieve data.
Digital circuits: Logic gates are used in digital circuits to control the flow of electrical signals and to implement Boolean logic functions.
Control systems: Logic gates are used in control systems such as traffic lights, elevators, and other automation systems to make decisions and control the movement of various components.
Communication systems: Logic gates are used in communication systems such as modems and routers to process and transmit data.
Automotive industry: Logic gates are used in the automotive industry to control the various systems such as engine, braking, and lighting system
Household appliances: Logic gates are used in household appliances such as washing machines, microwave ovens, and televisions to control their functions and features.
Medical equipment: Logic gates are used in medical equipment such as imaging machines and diagnostic equipment to control their functions and process data.
Real-life applications of logic gates are:
NAND gates used in burglar alarms and buzzers.
They are also used in push button switches, and functioning of street lights.
AND gates used to enable/inhibit the data transfer.
